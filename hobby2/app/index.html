BOULDERING

This first type of climbing is actually my favorite. This is how I started.
Bouldering is when you climb without a rope or harness. This can both be done indoor and outdoor. You need a spotter and a mat to catch your fall when it happens since you're not on a rope.
bouldering problems (the path that a climber takes in order to complete the climb) are usually less than 20 feet tall.

TOP ROPING

This is the most common type of climbing. Top Roping is when you climb with a rope that's attached to you and runs from belayer who would stand and the foot of the route throught one or more caribiners connected to an anchor system.
This is the type of climbing that most beginners choose to do because it is easier to star off with and you're (usually) in the hands of a more experienced climbing that's your belayer.

LEAD CLIMBING

Lead climbing is a more advanced type of climbing because it requires a little more skills than just being able to hold on to the wall.
Lead climbing is a climbing technique used to ascend a route. This technique is predominantly used in rock climbing and involves a lead climber attaching themselves to a length of dynamic (elastic) climbing rope and ascending a route while periodically attaching protection (quickdraws or traditional protection) to the face of the route and clipping into it.

SOLO CLIMBING

Solo climbing or soloing is a style of climbing in which the climber climbs alone, without somebody belaying them. That is exactly what it sounds like, you climb up with no ropes and if you take a fall...well you take a fall.
Anohter one of my favorite types of climbing is Deep-water soloing. This is when the climber climbs on a rock in the ocean and when you take a fall, you fall safely into the sea. Super fun on a hot hot day.
